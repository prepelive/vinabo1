Landsteiner's Law

Rule 1

If antigen is present, corresponding antibody is absent.

ABO & Rh Grouping Steps

1. Preparation (‡§§‡•à‡§Ø‡§æ‡§∞‡•Ä)

  • Label 5 tubes (A, B, D, A-cell, B-cell).
  • Prepare 5% RBC suspension (Saline ‡§Æ‡•á‡§Ç).

2. Reagent Adding

  • Forward (A, B, D): 1 drop Antisera + 1 drop 5% RBCs.
  • Reverse (A-cell, B-cell): 2 drops Serum + 1 drop 5% known cells.

3. Observation (‡§Ö‡§µ‡§≤‡•ã‡§ï‡§®)

  • Centrifugation (‡§Ö‡§™‡§ï‡•á‡§Ç‡§¶‡•ç‡§∞‡§£): Spin (1500 rpm, 1 min).
  • Observation: Gently resuspend cell button.
  • Check: Look for Agglutination (‡§ó‡•Å‡§ö‡•ç‡§õ‡§®).
  • Rh Neg Confirmation: Incubate at 37¬∞C, wash, add AHG (IAT).

Common Pitfalls (‡§ó‡§≤‡§§‡§ø‡§Ø‡§æ‡§Å)

  • Clerical Error: Wrong sample/labeling. (Fix: Double-check ID)
  • Contamination: Dirty glassware. (Fix: Use clean tubes)
  • Reagent Error: Expired reagents. (Fix: Run daily QC)
  • Centrifugation: Over/under-spinning. (Fix: Calibrate)

Interpreting Results (‡§µ‡•ç‡§Ø‡§æ‡§ñ‡•ç‡§Ø‡§æ)

  • Agglutination: Positive (+) reaction
  • No Agglutination: Negative (0) reaction
  • Hemolysis (RBCs burst): Positive (+) reaction
  • Rouleaux: False positive (Fix: Saline wash)

Core Definitions

  • Antigen (‡§è‡§Ç‡§ü‡•Ä‡§ú‡§®): Substance on RBC surface (e.g., A, B, D).
  • Antibody (‡§è‡§Ç‡§ü‡•Ä‡§¨‡•â‡§°‡•Ä): Protein in serum (e.g., Anti-A).
  • Agglutination: Clumping of RBCs.
  • Antisera (‡§è‡§Ç‡§ü‡•Ä‡§∏‡§ø‡§∞‡§æ): Readymade antibodies for testing.

Key Rules

  • Forward + Reverse grouping must match.
  • Rh Control tube must be negative.
  • Universal Donor: O Negative (O-)
  • Universal Recipient: AB Positive (AB+)

Mistake Bank (Error Traps)

  • Weak D: May type as Rh-neg in saline. Needs IAT.
  • Bombay Group: No A/B/H antigen. Shows 'O' but has Anti-H.
  • Cold Agglutinins: False positive in reverse. (Fix: Warm sample)

3-Line FAQ (Viva Qs)

  • Q: What is a 5% RBC suspension?
    A: A standard dilution of RBCs in saline for tube testing. Ensures optimal antigen-antibody ratio.
  • Q: Why no reverse group for Rh?
    A: Rh antibodies (Anti-D) are 'immune' (not natural), so they aren't normally present in Rh-negative people.
